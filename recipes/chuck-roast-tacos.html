Chuck Roast Tacos Recipe

Return to Home Page

Description

This recipe will result in juicy, flavorful chuck roast tacos feeding 6+ people per serving. This is one of my go-to, easy to make weekly recipes. Authentic? I don't know, but I do know it yields a large amount of food for small amount of effort. This isn't some copy and paste recipe for the sake of The Odin Project, but a recipe created over time from trial and error. I loved cooking far before I found a love for learning programming. Enjoy! Feedback will always be welcome!

Ingredients List

  • Chuck roast (about 2.5lbs)
  • White Onions (4-6 medium)
  • Beef Stock (48oz)
  • Chili Powder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Smoked Paprika
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Thyme
  • Bay leaves
  • 1-2 bunches of cilantro (optional)
  • Salsa
  • Corn tortillas
  • Shredded Cheese
  • Butter
  • Olive Oil and/or Vegetable Oil

Steps

Cooking the chuck roast

  1. Half moon all of the onions
  2. Heat a meadium to large pan over medium-high heat
  3. Put half of the onions and all of the beef stock in a large crock pot and place on low heat
  4. Combine in a small dish chili powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper generously. This will be used to coat the chuck roast before searing
  5. After seasoning is blended together, cover the chuck roast generously in all crevices, sides, and edges
  6. Put oil in pan and sear the chuck roast about 3-4 minutes per side and each edge for about 45 seconds to 1 minute
  7. Place the chuck roast in the crock pot and put the other half of the onions over the top. Note: I love the onions that come out of this recipe, so feel free to add or take away based on personal preferance or crock pot size. For my crock pot, I have to use tongs to adjust the onions of the course of the cook.
  8. Add thyme and about 2 bay leaves
  9. Turn the crock pot to low for about 6-7 hours until the chuck roast is tender and falls apart
  10. Optional: Chop the cilantro

Making the tacos

  1. Heat a small pan on medium heat
  2. To cook the corn tortillas: heat one side, flip, rub with a stick of butter and flip again. Add cheese
  3. Once cheese melts fully, or even slightly, remove from heat
  4. Repeat until desired number of tortillas are created
  5. Shred the chuck roast and add small amount to tacos
  6. Top with cilantro and salsa
  7. Note: you can make rice to add into tacos or on the side. I will typically use minute rice jasmine rice cups, and sometimes even crisp up some hashbrowns.
  8. Bonus note: the broth from the chuck roast and onions makes for some great ramen!
